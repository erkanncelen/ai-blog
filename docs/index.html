Also, I used to think that one day I might get someone to iron my shirts, but the truth is I really like doing them myself.

Also, I used to think that one day I might get someone to iron my shirts, but the truth is I really like doing them myself. It's a very personal thing to do, and when you're done ironing you don't want to get yourself thrown on the fire. There were two places I'd had trouble with trying to iron my shirts. First was the shirt where there was a huge chunk of the cloth. I had to put some stuff onto it before it melted, so I put it onto the shirt itself. Second was another place I had trouble ironing shirts. You could easily add some tape to it, but the tape was too small to keep up. There was still a lot of material left to iron, and I just started doing a little bit of stretching. When I tried to iron it I could just sit down and stretch it for 3½ to 5 minutes, but then I would have to push it down again, then push it again every time I'd gotten hard enough to stretch. That's where the problem started. In the last 5 minutes I had to get some stuff to move around in my shirt's pockets. I had to take a lot out, and then I'd have to throw it around a lot. So in the end I had to iron my shirts again. I didn't expect to have a problem ironing.
