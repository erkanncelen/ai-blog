It is, I claim, nonsense to say that it does not matter which individual man acted as the nucleus for the change.

It is, I claim, nonsense to say that it does not matter which individual man acted as the nucleus for the change. It is just that in their minds, it was not their particular mind-set that had changed the thing. They were determined to live in the same body—to be able to make the changes that they wanted to make, while in reality, each of those changes was a part and parcel of the whole, and all this was something to be taken into account in the equation and made a part of the whole for every individual being involved in the process. One of the few things about where I have found out about the mind-set of the young, I have not found out quite as well as I might. I have studied most of the things, and I have been told that many of the factors of how the mind-set has changed are still not in our own minds. Those who have been studying the mind-set of the young can see the problem. When we have some understanding of the mind-set of the young it is sometimes difficult to understand why we have changed them, what is the result? How can we know the mind-set of a subject? It is our right to know the mind-set of our subjects, but how do we know that? We have had to come up with theories of many different kinds, and I think that many of them are at best meaningless if we don't understand them.
